Week 1: Getting the lay of the land

Posted Monday, Week 1 · Internship Reflection

the seeking part

I'm continuing on as an IT Intern at Emplify Health in Green Bay. Since I was already working there, this first week was less about the job search and more about starting the internship officially and getting set up in a new capacity.

the work

The first week was mostly onboarding-adjacent: completing corporate orientation, going through HIPAA training, and touring the clinics and the office I'll be working out of. My technical training included getting reacquainted with the ticket queue, working through some device deployment tasks, and spending time in Intune, SCCM, and imaging.

One thing that stuck with me this week: how every IT professional has their own way of doing things, and how many different techniques there are to solve the same issue.

the experience

It was really cool using my skills in a real work environment and seeing firsthand how things run. One thing that surprised me is how much software and how many computers it takes to run an organization of this scale. What was harder than expected is the learn-as-you-go nature of it all — there's no catalog you can look through for answers, and a lot of the day-to-day problems you run into are new ones you just have to work through as you go. Something I'm looking forward to as I keep learning here is getting really comfortable with AD, Intune, and SCCM, since those feel like valuable transferable skills.

mini tutorial / example

A few things I worked on this week: assisted with tickets, troubleshot a printer, helped with cable patching, and collaborated with networking to open some ports. looking ahead
